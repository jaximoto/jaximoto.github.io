Polar Ascent

Introduction

Polar Ascent is the result of a 3 day game jam, the theme was polarity, so my team decided to express it through the duality of fire and ice. This is a 2D platformer where the player utilizes the power of fire and ice to tackle the obstacles in front of them and climb to the top of the mountain.

Contribution

During this project, I fulfilled the role of lead developer/scrum master. I worked with 2 other programmers and an artist, if anyone had a question about what they were working on or trouble implementing a feature, I would context switch to provide support. So this caused me to have an indirect hand in nearly everything in the game. However, I was directly responsible for the player animation, controls, and physics. I also implemented the UI and created the workflow for our level designer.

Player Movement

Since this was a 2D platformer, I wanted the character to feel fairly smooth to control and push myself out of my comfort zone. It may be slightly overtuned, but I implemented a jump buffer, custom jump gravity, coyote time, and head bump smoothing/corner correction.

Player Animation

As for the player animation, I had to work with a small number of frames for each animation due to time constraints, but I feel they still look okay. I implemented running, jumping, falling, and dying animations for the player. When the player changes modes, we chose to change the player's sprite entirely instead of a simple color change. Which caused us to copy and paste the same animations with different sprites. Luckily, Unity has an animator override controller, allowing us to re-purpose the same logic with different animation frames. If presented with a similar problem for future game jams, I will likely push for a simple palette swap rather than duplicating the same animation for slightly different sprites, as it can save time and lower complexity.

Retrospective

For only 3 days, I feel that our team did a pretty good job, as our team tackled worked with a lot of systems that we aren't familiar with, and this was our first 2D platformer. Next time, we will likely spend more time applying juice to the game through music, sfx, and vfx. Although our our team fell victim to scope creep, this project was a great step forward in gaining new skills and sharpening our knowledge of game development. I'm super excited for the next one!
